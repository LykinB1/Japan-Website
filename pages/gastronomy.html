Sushi

  • 1. The famous Japanese dish: sushi. This dish was created by chance when they were trying to solve a problem: preserving fish.
  • 2. Sushi is made with seasoned rice topped with raw fish.
  • 3. Although sushi is a tipical dish from Japan, it's origin isn't there. The first sushis were made in Southeast Asia and then in China (200 B.C.).
  • 4. There isn't any sense to it, but it is very probable that sushi started the known "Fast Food" trend in Japan.

Ramen

  • 1. Ramen is a noodle dish with many complex ingredients. It's origin maybe is in China but some people says the Ramen's origin is much more complex.
  • 2. The Ramen Museu tells us that the first Japanese to eat ramen was a man called Tokugawa Mitsukuni in 1660. This man ate ramen after chat with a Chinese friend and he suggested for add some ingredients to the Soba soup.
  • 3. Ramen is made with noodles, broth (usually miso), Tare sauce, and some other ingredients: slices of pork, eggs, seaweed, and green onions.
  • 4. Ramen not always was famous how is today, its popularity was due to the ease of preparing the dish.

Tempura

  • 1. Tempura is a frying technique. Various types of ingredients are used, such as seafood or vegetables.
  • 2. Although it is known as an oriental cuisine, Tempura originated in Portugal and was created by Catholics who did not eat meat during Holy Week.
  • 3. Tempura is simple to prepare. First you have to organize the vegetables that will be used as ingredients and then prepare the batter. Tempura batter is made with just cold water and flour. The next step is simply frying.
  • 4. There's not much more to say about tempura, but one curiosity that might be useful is that to get a good crunch, the batter must be well chilled before frying.

Tonkatsu

  • 1. Tonkatsu is a dish with breaded pork cutlet and curry broth.
  • 2. Tonkatsu was created in Japan Meiji era (1868-1912)
  • 3. Tonkatsu is made with a pork fillet breaded in oil, along with rice and curry broth. The curry is prepared with meat stock dissolved in water, vegetables and can also sometimes include meat.
  • 4. Tonkatsu has several variations depending on the region of Japan in which it is prepared.
