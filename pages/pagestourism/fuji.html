HAKONE

Hakone city is located in the western region of Kanagawa Prefecture and is part of the Fuji-Hakone-Izu National Park.


The prefecture is known both within and outside Japan as a resort area filled with high-quality hot springs and stunning views of Mount Fuji.


Regardless of the season, the iconic Mount Fuji can be admired from Hakone. Moreover, the region is blessed with breathtaking pristine nature. If Mount Fuji represents the heart of the nation, then nature is its soul.

Main Attraction

Mount Fuji

At 3,776 meters, Mount Fuji is the highest peak in Japan, the result of volcanic activity that began approximately one hundred thousand years ago. Currently, Mount Fuji and its surroundings are a popular recreational destination for hiking, camping, and relaxation.

One of the most emblematic symbols of the country, travelers from around the world head to the provinces of Shizuoka and Yamanashi to visit this inspiring mountain. For the Japanese, however, Mount Fuji has long been considered a place of spiritual significance and a source of artistic inspiration.
